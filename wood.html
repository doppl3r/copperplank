European oak

A grand species that derived its name from the Latin word for "strength". This exceptionally hard and resilient wood has a course texture, an open pore structure and creamy white complexion. It grows slowly in colder climates which make for a tight, straight grain and is characterized by a center cathedral grain.

Many European Oak floor products are actually manufactued in Asia and are made with inferior quality raw material because it is cheaper. Cost cutting to increase profits leads to raw material that include extensive splits which require significant amounts of wood fill and yield a less stable product. This means a lower quality product and no cost savings because of the many layers of middle men.
